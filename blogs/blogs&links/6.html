7 Natural Home Remedies to Have Sound Sleep

Have you been awake until 2 AM daily, or are you one of the people throwing up in bed? The National Sleep Foundation reports that 30% to 40% of adults suffer from occasional insomnia, and 10% to 15% have sleep problems. Adults need seven to eight uninterrupted hours of a good night’s sleep. The market is flooded with sleep aids and medications, making it tempting to pop a pill to solve your sleep problems. There are also many natural remedies for deep sleep available. Consistency and lifestyle changes are usually involved in this approach. This article provides you with some natural sleep remedies that can help you improve your sleep quality.



Home Remedies for Good Sleep

Try some of the below-mentioned home remedies to get a sound sleep every night:

1. Warm Baths

If you have insomnia, take a hot shower two hours before bedtime. Studies have shown that insomniacs who take a bath before sleeping, sleep better than those who do not. Bathing in hot water relaxes your body and soothes your nerves. Add chamomile, rosemary, or lavender essential oils to the bath water to make it more soothing.

2. Home Massage

You can calm your mind and body with massage therapy. Massage at night can reduce pain, anxiety, and depression and improve sleep quality. Home massages do not require professional assistance; you can do them yourself.

3. Sleep-promoting Foods

Some foods that help you sleep if you take them before bedtime are:
* Pre-bedtime Milk: Melatonin, the hormone that induces sleep, is produced in the body when milk is consumed. To relax your mind and body, drink warm milk before bed. Tryptophan in milk promotes sound sleep. Put some cinnamon powder in a glass of boiling milk. Before going to bed, drink it and sleep well every night.
* Apple Cider Vinegar: A cup of apple cider vinegar contains amino acids that help relieve fatigue and ensure a proper sleep cycle. Mix two spoons of honey and apple cider vinegar in a glass of warm water. Try this water before going to bed.
* Herbal Tea: Taking herbal tea is among the best Indian home remedies for good sleep at night. It is well known that chamomile tea induces sleep and is a mild tranquilliser. To enjoy tea’s soothing and relaxing properties before bedtime, Indian culture suggests brewing a hot cup. Add honey to your tea for sweetness and benefits. Herbal teas generally increase body heat, which makes you sleepy.
* Cumin Seeds: Cumin induces sleep and promotes digestion, preventing stomach upset and indigestion. Cumin tea is an excellent natural sleep aid before bed. Mix cumin powder with bananas or add it to your dinner for a double benefit.
* Limit Caffeine: Identifying the foods you should limit to getting proper sleep is equally important. You may aggravate your sleep disturbance symptoms if you consume caffeine-containing beverages and foods. The Ayurvedic system suggests eliminating all caffeinated products from your diet if you have chronic insomnia. If you have mild or acute insomnia symptoms, you should stop using caffeine by dusk.



4. Create a Sleep Pattern

Establishing a sleep-wake cycle may assist your body in developing a healthy sleep routine and resting properly. Additionally, it may improve the quality of your sleep. Keeping the same time for waking up and going to bed every day will help you develop a sleeping pattern.
